About Us

Computational Research in AI, Data Science, and Ledger Engineering

🌱 CRADLE Lab

CRADLE Lab is an interdisciplinary research group committed to advancing intelligent, transparent, and secure digital systems. We focus on converging Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and Distributed Ledger Technologies (DLT) to address real-world challenges across sectors such as fintech, healthcare, education, and cyber-physical systems.

  • Founded by Dr. Usama Arshad, with a mission to build scalable, impactful computational frameworks.
  • Bridging AI and Blockchain for secure, explainable, and decentralized solutions.
  • Promoting real-world applications from data privacy to digital identity and intelligent automation.

Our core research spans: 🤖 Artificial Intelligence (ML, DL, XAI), 📊 Data Science (Predictive Analytics, Visualization, Privacy), and 🔗 Ledger Engineering (Blockchain, Smart Contracts, Decentralized Identity).

We welcome collaborations with students, academics, and industry professionals through thesis supervision, internships, and joint research.

Research Areas

Our lab explores diverse domains across AI, Data Science, and Blockchain Technologies

Artificial Intelligence

Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Explainable AI, and autonomous intelligent agents.

Data Science

Predictive analytics, visualization, big data systems, and privacy-preserving computation.

Ledger Engineering

Blockchain protocols, smart contracts, decentralized identity, and distributed systems.

Security & Trust

Cybersecurity, zero-knowledge proofs, secure AI architectures, and trusted data exchange.

  • Blockchain-Driven CPS Testing

    Hybrid testbeds and secure validation protocols for cyber-physical systems.

  • AI with Privacy by Design

    Model pipelines that embed privacy-preserving computation and federated learning.

  • DNA Ledger Architecture

    Combining DNA storage and blockchain to secure genetic data at scale.

  • Secure Identity Management

    Decentralized identity solutions using verifiable credentials and ZKPs.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How can I become a member of CRADLE Lab?

    Anyone from any institution can join CRADLE Lab by either co-authoring a research paper with our team or by completing our official training program. Open collaboration is encouraged.

  • Are the lab’s projects and code open to the public?

    Yes, most of our research projects are publicly available. Visit the projects section for GitHub repositories, datasets, and documentation. Some collaborations may require access approval.

  • Do I need to be affiliated with a university to contribute?

    No formal affiliation is required. We welcome researchers, students, and professionals globally who are passionate about applied research and real-world innovation.

  • What are the core domains CRADLE Lab focuses on?

    We work at the intersection of Blockchain, Artificial Intelligence, Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), and EdTech, focusing on practical applications and secure systems.

  • Does CRADLE Lab organize events or trainings regularly?

    Yes, we conduct seasonal bootcamps, workshops, and webinars that are open to all. These often include certifications, networking, and research opportunities.

  • Can I pitch or propose my own project idea to CRADLE Lab?

    Absolutely! We actively encourage project proposals. If your idea aligns with our mission, we’ll provide guidance, co-authorship opportunities, and technical mentorship.
